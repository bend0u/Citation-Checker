Topic 5: Generative Information Retrieval for Science
This project addresses a critical problem in AI for science: Can we trust the answers from AI
research tools? Generative search engines and AI research assistants promise to find and
synthesize information from scientific sources, but they often show weak or misleadingly wrong
citations. First, develop a method to improve generative information retrieval of scientific
information that can improve citation accuracy. This may require solving the provenance
problem: a systematic breakdown in the chain of scholarly acknowledgement that current
ethical frameworks fail to address. Finally, create a SaaS tool for scientific information retrieval.
1. Open-source AI tool beats giant LLMs in literature reviews — and gets citations right, E.
Gibney, Nature, Feb. 2026: https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00347-9
2. LLM use in scholarly writing poses a provenance problem, Brian D. Earp et al, Nat Mach
Intell 7, 1889–1890, 2025, https://www.nature.com/articles/s42256-025-01159-8
3. DeepTRACE: Auditing Deep Research AI Systems for Tracking Reliability Across Citations
and Evidence, Pranav Narayanan Venkit et al, 2026 https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04499
